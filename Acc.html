üßæ What is Accountancy?

Accountancy is the systematic process of recording, classifying, summarizing, and interpreting financial transactions of a business. It not only records what has happened but also provides insights to help in decision-making.

  • Track Performance: Evaluate profit or loss over a period.
  • Financial Position: Determine assets, liabilities, and equity.
  • Decision Making: Provide information for managers, investors, and regulators.
  • Legal Compliance: Ensure adherence to Accounting Standards and laws.
Example: If a firm purchases goods worth ‚Çπ50,000 on credit, Accountancy ensures proper entry in Purchase A/c and Creditor A/c, helping in accurate financial statements.

üìö Why is Accountancy Important?

Accountancy is crucial because it:

  • Supports Decisions: Helps management plan budgets, investments, and expansions.
  • Maintains Transparency: Ensures accurate reporting to investors, banks, and government.
  • Analyzes Performance: Ratios like Current Ratio, ROCE, and Debt-Equity Ratio help assess efficiency.
  • Preserves Historical Records: Documents transactions for audits, taxation, and legal purposes.
