Extra Theory Questions

1. Main Features of Partnership

  • Agreement: Formed on mutual consent.
  • Number of partners: Min 2, max 10 (20 in banks).
  • Profit sharing: Partners share profits and losses in agreed ratio.
  • Mutual agency: Every partner acts as agent of firm.
  • Unlimited liability: Partners are personally liable for firm debts.
  • Legal status: Not a separate legal entity.
  • Voluntary association: Partners voluntarily associate.

2. Partnership Deed â€“ Definition & Contents

A partnership deed is a written agreement specifying the terms, duties, and rights of partners.

  • Name & address of firm & partners
  • Nature & place of business
  • Duration of partnership
  • Capital contribution
  • Profit sharing ratio
  • Interest on capital & drawings
  • Partnerâ€™s salary, commission, allowance
  • Admission, retirement, death of partners
  • Dissolution terms

3. Goodwill â€“ Definition & Admission Treatment

Goodwill: Value of reputation & intangible benefits that help earn excess profits.

  • Valued and credited to old partners in profit sharing ratio.
  • New partner compensates old partners.
  • If no goodwill account, adjustments through capital accounts.

4. Mutual Agency â€“ Definition & Significance

Each partner is an agent of firm and other partners; their acts bind all.

  • Enables smooth business operation.
  • Allows delegation of work.
  • Increases risk of unauthorized acts.

5. Capital Account & Current Account

  • Capital Account: Permanent investment brought in by partner.
  • Current Account: Tracks ongoing transactions: profit share, salary, interest, drawings.

6. Sacrificing Ratio

Old partners give up profit share in favor of new partner.

Sacrificing Ratio = Old Ratio â€“ New Ratio

7. Interest on Drawings

  • Credited to P&L A/C as income
  • Debited to partnerâ€™s current account
  • Compensates firm for early withdrawal

8. Retirement of a Partner

  • Partner leaves voluntarily or by agreement.
  • Goodwill share compensated by continuing partners in gaining ratio.
  • Adjusted via capital accounts.

9. Duties of Partners

  • Act in good faith
  • Disclose material facts
  • Avoid conflict of interest
  • Share profits & losses fairly
  • Not compete with firm
  • Manage business diligently
